# Research Brief: Remote Work Productivity in 2025
**Date:** 2025-06-27

## Executive summary
The 2025 evidence points to a settled "hybrid by default" world rather than a swing
back to the office. Well-run remote and hybrid teams match or slightly beat fully
in-office output, but the gains depend heavily on management quality and async
practices rather than location alone. Disengagement — not location — is the real
productivity drag, and it shows up in both remote and office settings.

## Key findings
- Hybrid workers report the highest self-rated productivity, with fully-remote close
  behind and fully in-office lowest in several 2024–25 surveys [1][2].
- Output differences between remote and office teams are small once you control for
  role and management quality; the bigger driver is clear goals and async workflows [2].
- "Productivity paranoia" persists: a large share of managers doubt remote output even
  when the data doesn't support the doubt, fuelling unnecessary RTO mandates [3].
- Mandated full-time RTO is linked to higher attrition among senior and high-performing
  staff, partly offsetting any collaboration gains [4].
- Meeting overload and constant pings are now the most-cited productivity killers,
  ahead of "working from home" itself [1][3].

## Implications / what this means
- Leaders should manage outcomes, not presence — set clear goals and measure results.
- Invest in async-first practices (written updates, recorded demos) before debating days
  in the office.
- Before any RTO mandate, weigh the likely attrition cost against the collaboration gain.
