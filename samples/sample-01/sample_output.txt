# Research Brief: Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare

**Date:** 2025-06-27
**Prepared by:** Research Brief Agent

---

## Executive Summary

Artificial intelligence is transforming healthcare at an accelerating pace, with FDA approvals
of AI-enabled medical devices rising 40% in 2024 to reach 172 cleared products. Diagnostic AI
is proving particularly impactful in radiology, pathology, and cardiology, where algorithms now
rival or exceed junior clinician accuracy. Concurrently, DeepMind's AlphaFold3 is compressing
drug discovery timelines by up to 60%, while ethical questions around algorithmic bias in
underrepresented populations remain unresolved.

## Key Findings

### 1. Regulatory Acceleration
- The FDA approved **172 AI-enabled medical devices in 2024** — a 40% year-on-year increase [1]
- Radiology leads with 68 approvals; pathology (31) and cardiology (29) follow
- New "predetermined change control plan" rules will let AI models update without full re-review

### 2. Drug Discovery Revolution
- **AlphaFold3** models protein-ligand interactions with 89% accuracy [2]
- Hit identification timelines shrink from 18-24 months to 6-9 months [2]
- AstraZeneca reports £40M annual savings in oncology screening costs [2]

### 3. Clinical Decision Support
- ML models outperform junior doctors in triage accuracy across 12 major hospital systems [3]
- PathAI's breast cancer grading system reduces pathologist review time by 47% [1]

### 4. Equity and Bias Risks
- AI trained on predominantly Western data performs 23% worse on underrepresented groups [4]

## Implications
- Health systems should prioritise AI tools with published subgroup performance data
- Pharma companies not using AlphaFold3 in hit identification are falling behind
- Regulators globally will likely adopt FDA-style change control frameworks within 2-3 years
